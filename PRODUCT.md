# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

ISVOI serves buyers in Russia who are choosing curated Apple and mobile devices and want a clear, trustworthy path from discovery to purchase or trade-in. Users arrive from search, direct links, catalog browsing, and local recommendations; they need to understand what is available, why the device is safe to buy, and how to contact the store without friction.

## Product Purpose

ISVOI is a public storefront and content-managed catalog for verified devices. The site connects the brand promise, curated inventory, Store, Passport, Trade and Club surfaces, and lead intake into one reliable public experience. Success means users can quickly trust the offer, inspect devices, compare next steps, and submit a lead or continue to a purchase conversation.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and insider-friendly. The brand should feel premium and technically competent without becoming cold, overdesigned, or vague. The voice should make the user feel they are buying from people who know the devices and protect the buyer's time.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic electronics-store clutter, aggressive discount banners, anonymous marketplace noise, heavy casino-like promotions, fake luxury, and visual patterns that make verified devices feel like risky second-hand listings. Avoid purely decorative AI-style visuals when real product information, trust signals, and clear navigation are needed.

## Design Principles

1. Trust before spectacle: every visual and content decision should make device condition, provenance, availability, and next action easier to believe.
2. Premium through restraint: use clean hierarchy, quiet motion, precise spacing, and product-first imagery instead of loud decoration.
3. Fast orientation: header, catalog, device pages, and contact paths should answer "where am I, what can I do, why trust this?" within a few seconds.
4. Editor-safe surfaces: design decisions should survive Directus-managed content, including variable menu labels, logo lockups, captions, device names, and missing optional fields.
5. Mobile-first clarity: mobile users should be able to browse, compare, and start a lead flow without layout shifts or hidden critical actions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the working baseline for public pages: readable text contrast, visible focus states, semantic headings, accessible forms, keyboard navigation, reduced-motion respect, and responsive layouts that remain usable on small screens.
